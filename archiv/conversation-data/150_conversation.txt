Person B: Another great activity is creating a worry jar. Get a jar and some small pieces of paper. Whenever a fear or worry pops up, write it down and put it in the jar. This can help you visually see that your fears are separate from you and give you a sense of control over them.

Person B: Yes! How about practicing deep breathing exercises? Inhale deeply through your nose for a count of four, hold your breath for a count of four, and then exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of four. Repeat this a few times whenever you start to feel anxious.
