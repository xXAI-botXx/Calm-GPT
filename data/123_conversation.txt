Person B: It can be challenging initially, but with practice, it becomes easier. Do you want me to walk you through a simple meditation technique?

Person A: Yes, please. I'm willing to give it a try. What should I do?

Person B: Find a quiet and comfortable place to sit or lie down. Close your eyes and take a deep breath in through your nose, then slowly release it through your mouth. Let your body relax and release any tension.

Person B: Focus on your breathing. Feel the breath entering and leaving your body. As your mind starts to wander, gently bring your attention back to your breath. Imagine that with each breath, you're inhaling calm and exhaling fear.

Person A: I'm finding it difficult to keep my thoughts from drifting. What should I do?

Person B: Acknowledge any thoughts or worries that come up, but don't engage with them. Let them pass by like clouds in the sky. Bring your attention back to your breath each time. It's okay if your mind wanders. Just gently guide it back.
