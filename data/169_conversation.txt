Patient: What does cognitive-behavioral therapy involve?
Psychologist: Cognitive-behavioral therapy, or CBT for short, focuses on identifying and challenging negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to anxiety. It helps you develop coping strategies and new ways of thinking to manage and reduce anxiety over time. It is often an effective treatment for anxiety disorders.
Patient: That sounds promising. I'm willing to give it a try. How long does it usually take to see results?
Psychologist: The length of therapy can vary depending on the individual and the severity of the anxiety. Typically, people start noticing improvements within a few weeks to a few months of consistent therapy sessions.
